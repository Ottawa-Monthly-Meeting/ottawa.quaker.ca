Welcome to the Ottawa Monthly Meeting of the Religious Society of Friends (Quakers)

The Meeting house will be open for in-person Meeting for Worship from Sunday, Jan 29th, 2023 until further notice.

As an experiment, in-person Meeting for Worship will be held without remote technology on the third Sunday of February and March (Feb 19, and Mar 19).  Meeting for Worship will also be held on Zoom, but will not be connected to the Meeting House.

We welcome visitors of all ages.  (Scroll down below to find out how to get connected.) 

We have begun holding Blended Meeting for Worship beginning at 10:30 am on Sundays

Please note:  To attend in person, you must

- have at least 3 vaccinations against Covid-19

- wear a mask (there will be extras for anyone who doesn't have one)

- sit at social distance (we will mark chairs so you can see where you can sit)

- email the Clerk in advance at clerk.ottawafriends@gmail.com if you plan to come so we can save a place for you

We can accommodate only 12 people at social distance right now, so please RSVP every week that you wish to come, so that we know how many new folks can join us. 

We will continue to hold the midweek Meeting for Worship by Zoom only.

Wednesday Evenings with Ottawa Quakers

At 8pm on Wednesday evenings—following unprogrammed Meeting for Worship, which starts at 7pm—Ottawa Monthly Meeting (OMM) is offering 'Things of the Eternal.' During these sessions the presenter speaks about something important in their spiritual journey or practice, or on a particular aspect of Quaker process or history. All are welcome to attend the first hour of Worship and check-ins, or just 'Things of the Eternal' or both. Anyone with a leading to share on a topic dear to their heart is invited to contact Steve Fick. 

Please note, on the first Wednesday of the month, a ‘Meeting with a Chosen Focus’ is offered in place of Meeting for Worship and ‘Things of the Eternal,’ ‘Meetings with a Chosen Focus’ start at 7pm and last up to 2 hours. These sessions led by Lilia Fick are open to the broader Quaker community.

Information about the Wednesday evening sessions goes out regularly on our e-mail lists. If you need help getting connected, send an email to areoottawa2020@gmail.com.

We are amazed at what we can do on-line!

We are now gathering twice a week for worship (10:30 am on Sundays and 7 pm on Wednesdays), conducting monthly business meetings, and holding committee and special meetings all ON-LINE.  Although we miss being together in person, we are amazed at what we can do virtually.  We are also pleased that our virtual meetings are more accessible to people who have a difficult time joining us in person.

Everyone on the "minutes" e-mail distribution list should be receiving e-mails about these meetings.  If you aren't on this e-mail list of members and regular attenders, e-mail the webminder  and I'll help you get connected. 

It looks like we'll be in this pattern for a while.  If you are curious about Quakers in Ottawa, e-mail  areoottawa2020@gmail.com to reach our ARE&O (Adult Religious Education and Outreach) committee for more information.

Young Adult Friend Gatherings

Ottawa Monthly Meeting is currently blessed with an abundance of Young Adult Friends.*  Many of the YAFs are now meeting for a potluck about once a month.  If you self-identify as a YAF and aren't yet connected to this group, ask around on a Sunday or send the webminder an e-mail and I'll put you in touch. 

*Quaker-speak for people roughly in their 20's and 30's who have a connection to Quakers.

Quaker Quotes

It is our tender and Christian advice that Friends take care to keep to truth and plainness, in language, habit, deportment and behaviour; that the simplicity of truth in these things may not wear out nor be lost in our days, nor in our posterity's; and to avoid pride and immodesty in apparel, and all vain and superfluous fashions of the world.

—

Yearly Meeting in London, 1691
